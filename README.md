### FAQ
#### Q: What is a 5G mobile proxy?

A: A 5G mobile proxy routes your traffic through real mobile carrier IPs (3G/4G/5G), making requests appear as if they come from genuine mobile devices. Ideal for scraping, automation, and bypassing restrictions.

##### Q: Why use 5G over 4G or residential proxies?

A: 5G offers faster speeds, lower latency, and higher trust scores on platforms like social media or eCommerce sites. Mobile IPs are less likely to be blocked than datacenter or residential IPs.

##### Q: What are the top 5G proxy providers in 2026?

A: Decodo, Oxylabs, SOAX, Bright Data, and IPBurger lead in reliability, speed, and IP pool size.

##### Q: How much do 5G mobile proxies cost?

A: Rotating proxies: $3.50–$20/GB. Dedicated proxies: $50–$300/month. Pricing depends on carrier, location, and session type.

##### Q: Can I target specific countries or cities?

A: Yes. Most providers offer country, city, ASN, and even ZIP code-level targeting.

##### Q: Are 5G proxies undetectable?

A: High-quality providers (Decodo, IPBurger) use real carrier IPs, minimizing detection and CAPTCHAs.

##### Q: What’s the best use case for 5G proxies?

A: Social media automation, ad verification, web scraping, and multi-accounting.

##### Q: Do providers offer trials?

A: Some (Decodo, DataImpulse) offer paid trials or money-back guarantees.

##### Q: How do I verify a proxy’s carrier IP?

A: Use IP lookup tools (ipinfo.io). A real mobile proxy will show a carrier like T-Mobile or AT&T, not a hosting provider.

##### Q: What’s the difference between rotating and sticky sessions?

A: Rotating changes IPs per request; sticky keeps the same IP for a set time. Use rotating for scraping, sticky for account management.
